Elephant figurine head with painted designs from Harappa
It is unknown whether elephants were domesticated in the Indus Civilization. However, one of the few elephant figurines from Harappa is a head with large stylized ears and red and white stripes painted across the face. This may mirror the custom of decorating domesticated elephants (red and white are common colors) for ceremonies or rituals that is still practiced in South Asia. Elephant bones have also been found at Harappa. Approximate dimensions (W x H(L) x D): 5.4 x 4.8 x 4.6 cm.-- Richard H. Meadow https://www.harappa.com/figurines/44.html
I submit that the early iconographic style of depicting गणेश in Hindu tradition is traced to Indus Script Cipher, as a signifier of ferrite ore and metal wealth of the artisan guilds of Sarasvati-Sindhu CIvilization. This submission is consistent with the meaning of the expression tridhātu in all the narratives and prayers of the Rgveda.I submit that this is also consistent with elaborations in सायणभाष्यम् |
त्रिधातु is गणेश Indus Script metaphor because the elephant trunk is a hieroglyph which signifies iron mineral resource wealth: karibha, ibha 'elephant' rebus; karba, ib 'iron'.
