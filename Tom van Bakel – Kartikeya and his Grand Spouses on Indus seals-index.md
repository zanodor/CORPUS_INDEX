The birth of Saraswathi
M-1425 At
On the right side of this tablet is a goddess lying upside down and giving birth to a crocodile. Today a crocodile is the mount of the river goddess Ganges or the water god Varuna, but at the time of the Indus Valley Civilization this animal represented the river goddess Yami or Valli. Of course, the birth of the river goddess is of great importance in the Indus Valley Civilization.
The birth goddess lies upside-down: in this way her legs more or less form a pitcher which is the symbol for a womb as well as the mother goddess Shashti (see below).
