Signifier – Indus Script hieroglyph: Ivory manǰī ʻduck’ of Meluhha
Signified – Meluhha rebus meaning: mã̄ jhī ‘Boatman of Meluhha’
mã̄ jhī -- Maritime seafaring merchants of Meluhha traded in ivory artifacts.
Such a merchant’s professional calling card is an Indus Script anthropomorph
with Brahmi inscription overlay on Indus Script inscription.



"Found jammed up against the eastern wall of the tomb was this ivory
bird figurine. The bird has a squared off tail and tufted crest,
reminiscent of a tufted duck (Aythya fuligula), described in Colin
Richardson's Birds of the United Arab Emirates (1990) as "a fairly
regular winter visitor.on creeks, ponds, lagoons and reservoirs in the
Northern Emirates". It is intriguing to think that Mesopotamian
references to imported ivory birds from Meluhha may indeed refer to
just search an object as this..“
Source: D.T. Potts, 2000, Ancient Magan: The secrets of Tell Abraq, p.131
https://www.academia.edu/1911284/Ancient_Magan_The_secrets_of_Tell_Abraq_2000_
