Sign 124 signifier of declivity and 1) sloped linear stroke; 2) sloped notch – ingots, implements made by blacksmith
kamari ‘declivity’ rebus: kamari ‘working of blacksmith’ – this is the superset
Subsets are:
dhāḷ 'slanted stroke' rebus: dhāḷako 'ingots’.
खाांडा khāṇḍā A jag, notch, or indentation (as upon the edge of a tool or weapon)
rebus: khaṇḍa 'implements’.
The hypertext composed of two hieroglyphs reads:
kamari ‘working of blacksmith’ -- dhāḷako 'ingots’ + khaṇḍa 'implements’.
Thus, the cryptic message is: ingots and implements are working of (products made
by) blacksmith

Sign 190The hieroglyph, 'four spouts in watery field' is a hypertext composed of two hieroglyphs: 1. count of four; 2. sprout. The Meluhha rebus expression reads: kor̤u 'sprout' PLUS gaṇḍa 'four' Rebus: kor̤u 'bar of metal' PLUS kaṇḍa 'equipment'. PLUS kolom 'three' Rebus: kolimi 'smithy, forge’
Vākya padīya, ‘sentence of word’ message is: kor̤u bars of metal and kaṇḍa implements (of OR made by) khātī ʻmember of a caste of wheelwrightsʼ; kammaṭa 'mint, coiner'; kamar 'blacksmith’ (working in smithy/forge)
Note: khātī ʻwheelwrightʼ may also signify Mint-master, śeṭh ‘guild-master’
