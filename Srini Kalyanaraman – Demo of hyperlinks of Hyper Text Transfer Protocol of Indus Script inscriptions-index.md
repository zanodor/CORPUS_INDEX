The idea of a hyperlink can be easily demonstrated using an Indus Script inscription.
This is a pectoral pendant, an inscription. m1656
Click on the ear, (i.e. mentally link to the Meluhha word) which pops up in
the onlooker's brain: Wg. kār ʻ ear ' (CDIAL 3056), This word is hyperlinked
to a similar sounding khār खार् । लोहकारः 'BLACKSMITH' (Kashmiri).
This is how meaning is messaged that the blacksmith's professional calling
card is further described on the image components, i.e. hieroglyphs such as
overflowing pot, sack on the shoulder etc. (These are similar hyperlinks to
mean लोखंड lōkhaṇḍa 'iron', metal equipment' and कण्ठालः
kaṇṭhālaḥ 'maritime, boat'). This is just a demo of a messaging system The
cipher of the writing, accounting system is complete to convey the
professional calling card of the pendant wearer.
