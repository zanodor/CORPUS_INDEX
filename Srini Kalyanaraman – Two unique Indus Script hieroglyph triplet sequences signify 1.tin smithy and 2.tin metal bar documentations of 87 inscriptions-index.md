Two unique Indus Script hieroglyph triplet sequences signify 1.tin smithy and 2.tin metal bar documentations of 87 inscriptions
On Indus Script Corpora, two unique hieroglyph triplet sequences occur as signature tune category expressions,ranku ko ̤ru kárṇaka 'tin metal bar scribe' (documented), ranku kolimi kárṇaka 'tin smithy scribe' (ducumented). These expressions are prefixed by other metalwork attributes.
Paul Yule presents evidence of metal bars used as ingots. http://crossasia-repository.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/510/1/yule_man_envir_2001.pdf This
complements his book: Paul Yule, 1989, The copper hoards of the Indian Subcontinent:
Preliminaries for an Interpretation, in: Jahrbuch des Romisch-Germanischen Zentralmuseums Mainz, 36, 1989 (1992).
