बलुतें balutēm n A share of the corn and garden-produce assigned for the subsistence of the twelve public servants of a village, for whom see below. 2 In some districts. A share of the dues of the hereditary officers of a village, such as पाटील, कु ळकरणी &c.
बलुतेदार balutēdāra, बलुता balutā, बलुत्या balutyā m (बलुतें &c.) A public servant of a village entitled to बलुतें. There are twelve distinct from the regular Governmentofficers पाटील, कु ळकरणी &c.; viz. सुतार, लोहार, महार, माांग (These four
constitute पशहली or थोरली कास or वळ the first division. Of three of them each is entitled to चार पाचुांदे, twenty bundles of Holcus or the thrashed corn, and the महार to आठ
पाचुांदे); कुां िार, चाम्हार , परीट, न्हावी constitute दुसरी or मधली कास or वळ, and are entitled, each, to तीन पाचुांदे; िट, मुलाणा, गुरव, कोळी form शतसरी or धाकटी कास or वळ, and have, each, दोन पाचुांदे. Likewise there are twelve अलुते or supernumerary public claimants,
viz. तेली, ताांबोळी, साळी, माळी, जांगम, कळवाांत, डवऱ्या , ठाकर, घडशी, तराळ, सोनार, चौगुला. Of these the allowance of corn is not settled. The learner must be prepared to meet with other enumerations of the बलुतेदार (e. g. पाटील, कु ळकरणी, चौधरी, पोतदार, देशपाांड्ा , न्हावी , परीट, गुरव, सुतार, कुां िार, वेसकर, जोशी; also सुतार, लोहार, चाम्हार , कुां िार as
constituting the first-class and claiming the largest division of बलुतें; next न्हावी , परीट, कोळी, गुरव as constituting the middle class and claiming a subdivision of बलुतें; lastly, िट, मुलाणा, सोनार, माांग; and, in the Konkan̤, yet another list); and with other accounts of the assignments of corn; for this and many similar matters, originally determined diversely, have undergone the usual influence of time, place, and ignorance.

Of the बलुतेदार in the Indápúr pergunnah the list and description stands thus:—First
class, सुतार, लोहार, चाम्हार , महार; Second, परीट, कुां िार, न्हावी , माांग; Third, सोनार, मुलाणा,
गुरव, जोशी, कोळी, रामोशी; in all fourteen, but in no one village are the whole fourteen to be found or traced. In the Panḍharpúr districts the order is:—पशहली or थोरली वळ (1st
class); महार, सुतार, लोहार, चाम्हार , दुसरी or मधली वळ (2nd class); परीट, कुां िार, न्हावी , माांग, शतसरी or धाकटी वळ (3rd class); कु ळकरणी, जोशी, गुरव, पोतदार; twelve बलुते and
of अलुते there are eighteen. According to Grant Duff, the बलतेदार are सुतार, लोहार, चाम्हार , माांग, कुां िार, न्हावी , परीट, गुरव, जोशी, िाट, मुलाणा; and the अलुते are सोनार, जांगम, शशांपी, कोळी, तराळ or वेसकर, माळी, डवऱ्यागोसावी , घडशी, रामोशी, तेली, ताांबोळी, गोांधळी. In many villages of Northern Dakhan̤ the महार receives the बलुतें of the first, second, and third classes; and, consequently, besides the महार, there are but nine बलुतेदार. The following are the only अलुतेदार or नारू now to be found;—सोनार, माांग, शशांपी, िट गोांधळी, कोर- गू, कोतवाल, तराळ, but of the अलुतेदार & बलुतेदार there is much confused intermixture, the अलुतेदार of one district being the बलुतेदार of another, and vice versâ. (The word कास used above, in पशहली कास, मध्यम कास, शतसरी कास requires explanation. It means Udder; and, as the बलुतेदार are, in the phraseology of endearment or fondling, termed वासरें (calves), their allotments or divisions are figured by successive bodies of calves drawing at the कास or under of the गाांव under the figure of a गाय or cow.)

Artisans and seafaring merchants of Sarasvati Sindhu Civilization
organized themselves into guilds which constituted the Commonwealth
called janapada. In this Commonwealth, all wealth realized from maritime
trade was deposited into the COMMON treasury to be shared equitably
among the citizens of the commonwealth. Each artificer was entitled to a
share of the Commonwealth in accumulated in the treasury, consistent with
śreṇidharma, i.e. coporate form guild regulations.
Indus Script Corpora provides readings of many artisans listed in
historical periods as बलुतेदार and अलुतेदार entitled to a share of the village
farm produce). The artisans identified in the Corpora of 8000 inscriptions
catalogued so far, are: लोहार, कु ळकरणी and पोतदार.
