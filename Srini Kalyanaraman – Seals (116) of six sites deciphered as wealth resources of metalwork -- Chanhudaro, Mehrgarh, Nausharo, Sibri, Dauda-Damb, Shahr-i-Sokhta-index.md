Introduction
This monograph completes the decipherment of 1347 new material presented in Corpus 3.2
The decipherment covers the Indus Script hieroglyphs on the following numbersof seals/seal impressions:
Chanhudaro: metal artifacts 2 Chanhudaro: seals 55 Mehrgarh 10 Nausharo 31 Sibri 4 Dauda-Damb 1 Shahr-i-Sokhta 13
Total 116
Thanks to Asko Parpola's team which has been consistent in their efforts to document inscriptions from Sarasvati-Sindhu Civilization.
A total of 1347 inscribed artifacts with many clearly identifiable hieroglyphs of Indus Script are demonstrated to be catalogues of minerals and metals worked in the sites of the Sarasvati-Sindhu Civilization.
The latest publication (2019) is titled: Asko Parpola, BM Pande and Petteri Koskikallio eds., 2019, Corpus of Indus Seals and Inscriptions Volume 3: New material, untraced objects, and collections outside India and Pakistan, Part 2 Shahr-i-Sokhta, Mundigak, Mehrgarh, Nausharo, Sibri, Dauda-damb, Chanhu-daro; Ahar, Balathal, Gilund; Kalibangan; Rojdi, Helsinki, Suomalainen Tiedeakatemia (in collaboration with Massimo Vidale & Alessandra Lazzari;Catherine & Jean-Francois Jarrige, Gonzague Quivron & Helene Tromparent; Museum of Fine Arts, Boston; Marta Ameri; and Gregory L.
