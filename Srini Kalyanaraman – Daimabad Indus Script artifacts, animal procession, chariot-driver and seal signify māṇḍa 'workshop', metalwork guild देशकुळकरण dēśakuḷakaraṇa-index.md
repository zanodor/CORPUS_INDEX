Zebu bulls draw the chariot: पोळ pōḷa m A bull dedicated to the gods, marked with a trident
and discus, and set at large; zebu (Marathi) Rebus: पोळ pōḷa 'magnetite, ferrite ore' dula 'two' rebus: dul 'metal casting'.
Two black drongo birds flank the axle: pōlaḍu, 'black drongo' rebus pōlaḍ 'steel'; dula 'two' rebus; dul 'metal casting' pōlāda 'steel', pwlad (Russian), fuladh (Persian) folādī (Pashto).पोलाद [ pōlāda ] n ( or P) Steel. पोलादी a Of steel. (Marathi) bulad 'steel, flint and steel for making fire' (Amharic); fUlAd 'steel' (Arabic).
Membrum virile of the charioteer is a hieroglyph: bhaṛkanu 'with raised membrum virile' has rebus bhaṭṭhā 'kiln', फड phaḍa 'cobrahood' rebus फड phaḍa 'metals manufactory'.
A horse is shown on the central pole of the chariot: sadassa 'a noble steed of the horse kind' (Pali) sadom 'horse' rebus: sadana 'seat, dwelling'. The animal may also signify khar 'onager' rebus: khār 'blacksmith'.
