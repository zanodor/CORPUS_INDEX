the metal product is the palm tree: tāṛ, tāla 'fan palm'. The
aquatic birs id kaRa which signifies karaDa 'hard alloy'.
Thus, the cargo on the boat comprises large metal ingots of
hard alloy. This rebus rendering indicates that the oxhide
shaped large ingot was called ḍhāla. This Indus Script
cipher signifies that an ox-hide ingot of Ancient Near East
was called ḍhāla 'a large metal ingot' -- a parole (speech)
word from Indian sprachbund (language union or speech
linguistic area) of the Bronze Age.
