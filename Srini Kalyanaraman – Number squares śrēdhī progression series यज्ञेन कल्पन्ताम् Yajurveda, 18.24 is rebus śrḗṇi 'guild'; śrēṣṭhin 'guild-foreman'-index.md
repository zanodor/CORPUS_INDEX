Number squares śrēdhī progression series येज्ञन
कल्पन्ताम् Yajurveda, 18.24 is rebus śrḗṇi 'guild';
śrēṣṭhin 'guild-foreman'
I submit that the recounting of this śrēdhī 'arithmetical progression of numbers' in vasordhārā mantra of Yajurveda is an affirmation of the śrḗṇi dharma of creating and sharing wealth in a commonwealth, creating and growing the wealth of a nation.
Thus, this mantra is a veneration of the social organization of śrḗṇi, the guild of artisans working with wealth resources. vasor-dhārā a f., ‘stream of wealth’, N. of a partic. libation of Ghṛta at the Agni-cayana, AV. ; TS. ; Br. &c.; of the wife of Agni, BhP. ; of the heavenly Gaṅgā, MBh. ; of sacred bathing-place, ib. ; of a kind of vessel, ib. vasor-dhārā and vasoṣpati a m., prob. ‘the god of wealth or property’ AV. i, 12 [Paipp. asoṣ-p°, ‘the god of life’].
This mantra is a veneration of vasoṣ-pati 'divinity of wealth or property'.

Seems to prove that going from the square of a number (n) to the square of the next number
(n+1), another number equal to 2n+1 must be added.
(n+1)^2-(n)^2=2*n+1(n+1)^2-(n)^2=2*n+1(n+1)^2-(n)^2=2*n+1
