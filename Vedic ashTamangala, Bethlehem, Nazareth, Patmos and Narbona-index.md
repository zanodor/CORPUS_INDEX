Theocracy(cf.vedic sruta raashTra/sanatana dharma) is the oldest
and overriding form of government in the world based on
religion,practised in the ancient and modern civilisations alike.
Whether autocracy, oligarchy or democracy,theocracy influences
the governments of nations of the world even today. The
theocrats establish their rule by propagating belief in royal solar
gods who have roles in creation,preservation and destruction of
the world and its beings. These gods control every phenomenon
in the world and every event in all individual’s lives. The
theocrats institute their laws in the name of the gods.Countless
rituals , sacrifices, fasts and feasts, said to please the gods, are also
instituted.These effectively control the individual’s life from
womb to tomb, from generation to generation ,making the
theocratic government perpetual(sanatana).
The individual is trained to become a hapless being who can do
nothing without the help and blessings of the all powerful,all
pervading, omniscient god.The individual has to pray daily to the
gods to give him long life,protection from dangers and diseases,
good health,good food, lot of wealth,victory over enemies,
beautiful wife,good children, abundant rains,good crops etc. The
rulers of the nations were considered the sons(ja) of the sun(ra)
god and the father of the nation.

modern-day scholars have designated as Nag Hammadi Codex II,

may have been Syria, where Thomasine(Cf.St.Thomas and
Roman Syrian Christians of Kerala) traditions were strong. The
introduction states: "These are the hidden words that the living
Jesus spoke and Didymos Judas Thomas(Didymos> Nasatyau/

widely thought that the document originated within a school of

The Gospel of Thomas ,unlike the canonical Gospels, is not
a narrative account of the life of Jesus; but consists
of logia (sayings) attributed to Jesus, sometimes stand-alone,
sometimes embedded in short dialogues or parables; 13 of its 16
parables are also found in the Synoptic Gospels. The text contains
a possible allusion to the death of Jesus in logion 65, paralleled in
the Synoptic Gospels), but does not mention his crucifixion,
his resurrection, or the final judgement; nor does it mention
a messianic understanding of Jesus,probably because of its
origin during the early reign of Augustus Caesar.
