The spoked wheel as an Indus Script hieroglyph is dated to ca. 5th millennium BCE, attested by two cire perdue castings of brone spoked wheels (referred to by British Museum as amulets). I submit that these are Indus Script hieroglyphs to signify gold-copper-smith workshop.
Mehergarh, 5th millennium BCE. 2.2 cm dia. 5 mm reference scale. Perhaps coppper alloyed with lead. It is remarkable, that this six-spolked cire perdue copper alloy wheels made in Mehrgarh becomes a hieroglyph of Indus Script.
