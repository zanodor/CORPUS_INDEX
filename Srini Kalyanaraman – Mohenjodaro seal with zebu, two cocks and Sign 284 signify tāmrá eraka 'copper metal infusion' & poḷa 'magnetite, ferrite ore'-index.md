Mohenjodaro seal with zebu, two cocks and Sign 284 signify tāmrá eraka 'copper metal infusion' & poḷa 'magnetite, ferrite ore'
Mahadevan and R. Balakrishnan see in a zebu field symbol Mohenjodaro seal, a reference to the name of the city as 'cocks-city'. I disagree with this reading. I suggest that the seal signifies wealth resources of: tāmrá eraka 'copper metal infusion' & poḷa 'magnetite, ferrite ore'. I submit that both Mahadevan and Balakrishnan focus on the cocks and ignore the dominant field symbol, the zebu, bos indicus, which occupies more than 90% of seal space.

Marshall seal No. 338 Mahadevan interprets the text message as: 'Cocks-city' -- a reference to Mohenjo-daro. Two cocks are shown together with a sign which is generally interpreted as a city. (Mahadevan, Iravatham. 2011. “Akam and pur\am : ‘Address’ signs of the Indus script.” International Journal of Dravidian Linguistics. 40 (1): 81-94, p.86). R. Balakrishnan calls the cocks "are probably the gamecocks of Mohenjodaro in a fighting-mode". (http://rmrl.in/wpcontent/uploads/2012/07/IRC-Bulletin-3.pdf, p.49; cf. Balakrishnan, R. 2010. “Tamil Indus?: Korkay, Vanji, Tondi in the North-West and a ‘Boneeating Camel’ in the Cankam text”. Journal of Tamil Studies. 77: 191-206.) If cock is signified by the word சகாழி kōḻi the name of Mohenjo-daro should read: சகாழி kōḻi ūr or சகாழி kōḻi kōḍu. Hurray ! Calicut, said to be given to the Samorin (ഏറനോടു ) with the word ഇനി ക ോഴി ൂ ുന്ന കേശം ഉുണ്ട land to the extent, to which a cock is heard KU. (Gundert. Malayalam) The field symbol of Mohenjodaro seal is a zebu, bos indicus. This is read rebus in Meluhha: प ला pōlā 'zebu, bos indicus' Rebus: pōḷa 'magnetite, ferrite ore'. Thus, together with the text of the Indus Scription on the Mohenjodaro seal signifies the wealth resources of magnetite/ferrite ore and kamsa 'bronze or bell-metal' alloys and metal castings of alloy metals.
