The fire god Agni appears to be the main deity of The Mature Harappa Civilization
From 3240 to 2160 BCE New Year began at the moment the sun appeared in Pleiades. These 1080 years roughly coincided with the Early and Mature Harappa Civilization, which lasted from about 3300 to 1900 BCE. Usually marriages were performed during the New Year Festivals and because Agni was the presiding deity of Pleiades he was the one who gave life during the wedding nights:
'(May) this Agni, the excellent one, (give) thee to-day life for (our) prayers; give us life that we may live long,'‚Äî(and,) 'Life-giving, Agni, be strong by Havis;
Sankhayana Grihya Sutra Khanda 19-25, Translated by Hermann Oldenberg
And that is one of the reasons why the fire god was so loved in The Mature Harappa Civilization.
When we want to recognize the different deities on the Indus stamps it is important to know that they cannot be known by their mount, because in the Indus Valley Civilization animals were not yet mounted. It turns out that the animals themselves embodied the deities, just as it is described in the Rigveda. For example, in hymn 4-16 it is said to Indra: 'Thou a wild elephant' and on the stamps the storm god is indeed depicted as an elephant.
