I submit that the expression mleccha-mukha is derived from Meluhha sprachbund, ‘speech union’ which has the rebus word for múkha ‘face’ is mũ h ‘face’ (Lahnda) rebus: mũ h ‘ingot’ PLUS mleccha, milakkha ‘copper’. Thus, the expression mleccha-mukha signifies ‘copper ingot’.Mleccha-mukha 'goat face' appears on thousands of inscriptions of Indus Script Corpora, signifying the importance of copper trade in creating the wealth of the nation.

The face of the animal is mleccha-mukha. I submit that the explanation for this compound expression is found in Indus Script Corpora which has thousands of inscriptions with this hypertext of goat-face.
The Meluhha rebus expression is: mleccha-mukha ‘goat face’ rebus: mleccha-muh ‘copper ingot’.Since copper ingots together with ornament gold constituted the major metallurgical wealth resources of Meluhha regions engaged in maritime trade of these resources, it is apposite to find a rational for the expression from the copper ingots traded. This also explains why a goat is carried by the Meluhha merchant on Shu-ilishu Cylinder seal.
