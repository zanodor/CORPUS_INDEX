Rebus rendering of the first three hieroglyphs: पाांड्या pāṇḍyā 'a village customs officer, scribe, accountant'.
I submit the anthropomorphic hieroglyph signifiers on Gardez Mahāvināyaka pratimā signify rendered rebus in Meluhha that Mahāvināyaka is 1. a customs officer, scribe, accountant, 2. working with ib, karba 'iron' panja 'kiln, furnace'; and 3. working in फडा 'metals manufactory, factory, guild'.
Hence, Mahāvināyaka is signified by the woollen garment pāṇḍva illustrated with a tiger cub pã̄ ḍā, pã̄ ḍyā with the rebus reading of पाांड्या pāṇḍyā 'customs officer, scribe, accountant'.
