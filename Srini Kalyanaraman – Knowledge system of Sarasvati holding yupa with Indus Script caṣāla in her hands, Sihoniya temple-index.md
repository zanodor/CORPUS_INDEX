Knowledge system of Sarasvati holding yupa with Indus Script caṣāla in her hands, Sihoniya temple
Sarasvati on caṣāla, 'snout' of Varāha, Khajuraho.
caṣāla is an Indus Script hieroglyph: caṣāla 'snout of boar' rebus: caṣāla 'godhuma, 'wheat chaff' ring atop yupa to yield carbon fumes which infuse into molten metal to harden it'. (Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa) Thus, iron becomes steel. This knowledge system is narrated in the sculpture.
Yupa from Rajasthan, Mathura, East Borneo Many yupas have been found from many parts of India and in East Borneo. The yupa is an octagonal pillar venerated in Skambha Sukta of Atharva Veda (10.7).
