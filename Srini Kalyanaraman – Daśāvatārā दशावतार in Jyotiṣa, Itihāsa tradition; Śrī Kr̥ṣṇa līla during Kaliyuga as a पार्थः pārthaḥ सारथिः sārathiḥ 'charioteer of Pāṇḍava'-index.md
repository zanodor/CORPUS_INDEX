From the Sun God the Incarnation of Rama, from the Moon that of K ̥rṣṇa, from Mars that of Narasimha, from Mercury that of Buddha, from Jupiter that of Vāmana, from Venus that of Paraśurāma, from Saturn that of Kūrma (Tortoise), from Rāhu that of Varāha [Boar] and from Ketu that of [Matsya] (fish) occurred. Of the Daśāvatārā दशावतार, four incarnations of Paramātmamśa [i.e. Rāma, K ̥rṣṇa, Narasimha and Varāha] are for the specific mission of destroying asura 'evil' and restore dharma.
Śrī K ̥rṣṇa avatāra has been precisely dated in itihāsa tradition; kiśora Śrī K ̥rṣṇa's killing of Kamsa--the raison d'etre of K ̥rṣṇa avatāra. Date is 18 Feb.3102 BCE; just as Narasimha avatāra is to kill Hiraṇyakaśipu; Varāha avatāra is to kill Hiraṇyākṣa. All the avatāra are to restore dharma, to remove evil rulers.
This date of 18 Feb. 3102 is derived from the date of killing of Kamsa.
