Shahitump lead weight with Indus Script hieroglyphs reinforces the metaphor of RV 9.97.16 of Soma bearing
घन ghana 'iron weapon'
--RV 9.97.16 in a ghana metaphor signifies Soma bearing an iron weapon; --a remarkable reinforcement of अंशुः aṃśuḥ cognate ancu 'iron' (Tocharian).
--Indus Script rebus rendering explains the metaphor of मधमक्षिका 'bee' in reference to pyrites.
This is an addendum to:

Griffith translation: RV 9.97.16 Pleased with us, Indu, send us as thou flowest good easy paths in ample space and comforts. Dispelling, as it were with a club, misfortunes, run over the height, run over the fleecy summit.
Wilson translation: 9.097.16 Gratified (with our praises), Indu, flow into the large (vessel), granting us riches coming by holy paths and easily acquired; beating down the ra_ks.asas on every side as with a club, flow in a stream upon the elevated fleece.
The club, according to सार्यणभाष्यम् refers to घनेव घनीभूतेन लोहमेर्यनार्यधेनेव , i.e. a weapon made of iron. This metaphor is a re-inforcement of the thesis advanced that Soma signifies अंशुः aṃśuḥ molten, flowing, मध-धातुः pyrites; cognate ancu (Tocharian) molten iron ore. loha m. n. red metal, copper, VS.; loha m. (in later language) iron (either crude or wrought) or steel or gold or any metal; a weapon (Monier-Williams)
