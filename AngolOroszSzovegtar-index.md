PREFACE
This concise English-Russian dictionary contains 1595 entries and is designed for the graduates of the Russian Arms Control Speaking Proficiency Course (RACSPC) at the Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA). Since in addition to their duties of military linguists at DTRA, they should often work as escort interpreters for the Russian inspectors, this dictionary may become rather helpful in their everyday talks, while escorting Russian inspectors around the base, to the stores, at the restaurants, and interpreting conversations (chit-chat) between the Russian and American inspectors after their work. The entries are topically arranged. Within each topic, they are arranged in the alphabetic order. A bold vowel means a stressed syllable.
