M-1186 A
This is one of the most fascinating seals from the Ancient Indus Valley Civilization: in the centre the god Murugan kneels and prays to the moon goddess Shashthi, and the river goddess Valli, here depicted as a sphinx, walks behind him. Beneath them there are the seven Krittika, the nurses of Murugan.
What is going on here, how is it possible that the great Murugan himself kneels and prays?
In the first place, we can infer from the Rig Veda that it was customary in ancient India to perform the sacrificial acts in the morning. And this is wat‚Äôs happening here: at dawn the couple Murugan and Valli are on the horizon and pray to Shashthi, who is high in the sky.
This is a photo of the Shashthi crescent early in the morning.
Because, with new moon the sun conjuncts the moon, which means that the sun and moon travel together through the sky and thus they appear together on the horizon early in the morning. And with a full moon, the sun and the moon are opposite each other, so when the sun rises in the east, the full moon sets in the west. But the moon on the sixth day of the moon month stands in between east and west, which means that at dawn the crescent moves in the middle of the sky. So, when the sun rises in Krittika, Murugan is on the horizon together with his spouse, the river goddess Valli, while the crescent of Shashthi is high in the sky.
