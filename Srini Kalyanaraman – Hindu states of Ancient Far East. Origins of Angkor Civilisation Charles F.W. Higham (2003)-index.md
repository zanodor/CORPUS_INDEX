George Coedes, the famous French epigraphist, wrote about the great world heritage monument called Angkor Wat (Nagara Vatika). He titled his work, in French: Histoire ancienne des États hindouisés d'Extrême-Orient, Hanoi, Imprimerie d'Extrême-Orient, 1944, [rééd. sous le titre : Les États hindouisés d'Indochine et d'Indonésie, Paris, De Boccard (Histoire du Monde, vol. 8), 1948. This meant 'Hinduised states of Indo-China and Indonesia (now called Southeast Asia)' a clear account of the influence of Hindustan in establishing state institutions in this Indian ocean region.
