Doms (Bengali: ড োম) are a Bengali Hindu caste found in large numbers
in Birbhum, Bankura and other districts in the western fringe of the Indian state of West
Bengal.Traditionally, Doms were basket-makers, cultivators, labourers and drummers; their
wives serving as midwives. The Domba or Dom (Sanskrit ḍoma, dialectally also Domaki,
Dombo, Domra, Domaka, Dombar, Dombari and variants) are an ethnic group, or groups,
scattered across India. In North India, the preferred self-designation is Dom. The
form ḍomba is Prakrit, while ḍoma and ḍumba are encountered in Kashmiri Sanskrit texts.
Derived from ḍoma is domaki, the name of a language spoken in a small enclave in Gilgit
Baltistan, Pakistan. It is also believed that the Dom or Domi people of the Middle East, in
addition to the Roma of Europe, are descendants of Domba, who were taken, or travelled,
to Sassanid Persia as servants and musicians.
